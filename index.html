Rules

A Pool table game for 2 - 4 Players.

Balls are placed in triangle for break as shown in the illustration:

Player 1 then picks either Red or Yellow
(or bigs or smalls depending on ball set) and breaks.

If Player 1 pots a ball of their chosen colour then they get another shot.
The colour they chose on their first shot is still the colour they must pot.
If they pot the opposite colour it is considered a foul.
If they pot the black they lose and are out (also see tiebreaker).
If they pot nothing it is then Player 2's turn.

Player 2 then chooses a colour and play continues for each player
until one colour of balls has been entirely potted. Then the player
who potted the final ball of that colour can pot the black.
However all other players may not pot the black until the alternate
colour has been fully sunk. Once both colours are completely sunk
all players may pot the black.

When on black no fouls are penalised. To pot the black legally and win,
a trickshot must be played. A trickshot is considered to be any shot that
results in the black going in any pocket after either the white ball or
black ball comes into contact with any cushion, or the black ball is potted
off contact with another colour ball. For added difficulty, players may
decide to call the pocket.

Fouls:

A foul is committed when a player:
  a. Pots a ball opposite to their chosen colour;
  b. Pots the black ball (this also results in elimination);
  c. Pots the white ball (the white is then returned to the D);
  d. Touches any ball with anything other than a cue;
  e. Touches any ball with the cue other than the white ball;
  f. or Knocks any ball off the table;
A foul is penalised by the offending player missing their next turn.

Tiebreaker:

A tiebreaker occurs when the black ball is sunk prematurely (e.g. a foul) or
the white ball is potted on the same shot as a legal black ball sink and
therefore leaves more than one player still playing (on some tables this
leaves the remaining players without access to the black ball, e.g. paid pub
tables).
To determine a single winner, the remaining players take turns (in reverse
play order) attempting to pot the white ball from the top of the table (from
the centre dot) off the back cushion, and back into either the left or right
top pockets.
